Our Story

From a small workshop
to a landmark.

Edmund Thorne arrived in Bangkok in 1991, trained at the London College of Fashion and apprenticed under the legendary Hong Kong tailor Mr. Wei Chun. He found in Bangkok a city of remarkable taste and ambition — and a gap for truly world-class bespoke tailoring.

In 1994, he opened a single-room atelier in Silom with one assistant and a bolt of fine English wool. Within five years, by word of mouth alone, Atelier Thorne had outgrown three locations and earned a reputation that stretched beyond Thailand.

Today, the atelier employs twelve master craftspeople, maintains relationships with over forty of the world's finest mills, and serves a global clientele from our flagship studio on Sukhumvit Road.

A timeline of milestones.

Edmund Thorne establishes the atelier in Silom with a focus on English-cut bespoke suiting for Bangkok's professional class.
1999

Word spreads to expatriate communities. The atelier begins serving clients from Singapore, Hong Kong, and Europe visiting Bangkok on business.
2006
New Flagship Studio
Relocation to a dedicated three-floor studio on Sukhumvit, featuring a fabric library of over 1,500 bolts and a private fitting suite.
2012
Ladies' Tailoring Programme
Launch of a dedicated women's tailoring service, led by head cutter Priya Memsri, recognising growing demand for precision womenswear.
2019
Second Generation
Edmund's son, Oliver Thorne, joins the atelier as Creative Director, bringing a refined contemporary sensibility to the heritage house.
2024
Thirty Years of Excellence
Atelier Thorne marks three decades with the launch of a limited anniversary collection and a new fabric sourcing partnership in Kyoto.

Fabric Guide

How to choose the right fabric.

🌡
Consider the Climate
Bangkok's heat calls for breathable fabrics like linen, tropical-weight wool, and fine cotton. Reserve heavier cashmere and tweed for travel garments or air-conditioned environments.
◈
Think About Occasion
For boardroom suits, a Super 120s or 150s wool offers the right authority. For weddings and formal events, silk blends and fine cashmere elevate the moment.
♻
Durability vs. Luxury
Higher twist numbers mean more durable, wrinkle-resistant cloth. Lower twist creates a softer, more luxurious hand. Our tailors will guide you to the right balance.
